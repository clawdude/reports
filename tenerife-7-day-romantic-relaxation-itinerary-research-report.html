7-Day Tenerife Romantic Relaxation & Sightseeing Itinerary

Created 3 May 2026 · Issue #7 · Objective: a week-long Tenerife plan balancing leisure, sightseeing, iconic experiences, and slightly secluded marriage-proposal spots.

Recommended shape: split the week between the south-west coast for easy beaches/sunsets and the greener north for old towns, Anaga, Garachico, and natural pools. Keep Teide for the clearest forecast day and reserve the proposal for a quiet golden-hour location rather than a crowded resort viewpoint.

Best base strategy
4 nights Costa Adeje/La Caleta/Los Cristianos area + 3 nights Puerto de la Cruz/La Laguna area. If using one base, choose Costa Adeje and accept longer north-coast drives.
Car advice
A rental car makes Masca, Anaga, Teide viewpoints, and secluded proposal backups much easier. Avoid night driving on mountain roads after a heavy dinner or wine.
Proposal recommendation
Primary: sunset above Masca/Teno area if conditions are calm. Backup: Punta de Teno lighthouse area or a quiet Teide stargazing stop.

Executive summary

  • Best rhythm: alternate one bigger scenic day with one slower beach/town day. Tenerife is compact, but mountain drives are tiring.
  • Iconic anchors: Teide National Park, Masca/Teno, Anaga Rural Park, San Cristóbal de La Laguna, Garachico, La Orotava, whale/dolphin watching, and a black-sand or natural-pool swim.
  • Not too mundane: each day has one memorable visual highlight, but avoids overloading the trip with back-to-back tours.
  • Proposal timing: do it on Day 3 or Day 5, not the last night. That leaves weather backups and lets the rest of the trip feel celebratory.

7-day itinerary

Day 1 Arrive + slow coast

Base: South-west coast. Plan: check in, decompress, walk La Caleta or Playa del Duque, and have a low-key seafood dinner near the water. If energy is good, sunset at Playa de la Enramada or a promenade stroll toward Costa Adeje.

Why: start relaxed instead of forcing a sightseeing sprint after travel.

Day 2 Ocean + resort-free sunset

Morning: responsible whale/dolphin watching from Los Cristianos/Los Gigantes area. Afternoon: beach time, pool, or a short swim/snorkel. Evening: Los Gigantes cliffs viewpoint, then dinner away from the busiest strips.

Proposal fit: visually dramatic, but often not secluded. Better as a romance-building day unless you find a quiet private boat option.

Day 3 Masca + Teno proposal window

Morning: drive early to Masca village via Santiago del Teide; stop for coffee and views. Midday: slow lunch and viewpoints, avoiding peak bus-tour times where possible. Golden hour: Punta de Teno lighthouse area or a quiet Teno/Masca overlook if access/weather allow.

Why: Masca is iconic and cinematic; Teno gives a remote Atlantic edge. Keep the day flexible because roads, clouds, and access rules can change.

Day 4 Garachico + La Orotava

Morning: transfer north with a stop at Garachico: old town, volcanic coast, and El Caletón natural pools if sea conditions are safe. Afternoon: La Orotava historic centre. Evening: Puerto de la Cruz dinner and slow walk.

Why: cultured, pretty, not too intense after the proposal day.

Day 5 Teide sunset / stargazing backup proposal

Morning: late start and leisure. Afternoon: drive into Teide National Park for Roques de García, crater landscapes, and sunset. Night: stargazing stop or guided stargazing if you prefer less logistics.

Proposal fit: excellent backup or even primary if you want epic rather than coastal. Choose a quieter pull-off/viewpoint, bring warm layers, and avoid the exact busiest sunset spot.

Day 6 La Laguna + Anaga

Morning: San Cristóbal de La Laguna, a UNESCO-listed colonial city, for architecture, cafés, and an unhurried stroll. Afternoon: Anaga Rural Park viewpoints and a short forest/coast walk, such as a mirador-focused route if you do not want a heavy hike. Evening: dinner in La Laguna or Santa Cruz.

Why: the green north-east contrasts strongly with the south and Teide.

Day 7 Choose-your-mood finale

Relaxed option: beach/natural-pool morning, long lunch, souvenir stroll. Sightseeing option: Santa Cruz + Playa de Las Teresitas. Celebration option: private spa, sunset dinner, or photoshoot in La Laguna/Anaga/Teide depending on where the proposal happened.

Why: protect the final day from overplanning and leave a buffer for weather-delayed must-dos.

Practical notes

  • Weather: Tenerife has microclimates. South can be sunny while Anaga/Teide are cloudy or cold. Decide Teide/Anaga days by forecast, not by rigid order.
  • Clothing: pack beachwear plus warm layers for Teide, Anaga, and sunset viewpoints.
  • Reservations: book whale watching, any Teide cable car/guided stargazing, and proposal dinner in advance.
  • Safety: avoid swimming in natural pools when surf is high; respect access signs around Teno, Teide, and mountain roads.
  • Photos: if the proposal matters photographically, hire a local photographer and tell them your backup spot, not just the dream spot.
